Show Tips for Beginners

  • Prepare your goat.

    • Before the show, you can try to bond with your goat by scratching it between the ears, brushing it, and feeding it out of your hand. You can also try letting it follow you on short walks.

  • Prepare you goats stomach.

    • You can feed your goat a little hay once or twice weekly to keep its rumen (stomach) working properly. However, you should avoid feeding your goat hay right before the show, as it can make your goat look overfilled.

  • Prepare fo the show ring.

    • Before showmanship, evaluate the show ring for low spots and try to position your goat so its front feet don't end up in them. You can also try to bring some tree shade to help protect your goat from heat, cold, and wind.

  • During the show.

    • When leading your goat, try to keep it between you and the judge, and switch sides as the judge moves. You can also try to keep your goat's front shoulder even with your leg and its head in front of your body. When the judge is in front of you, you can try to keep your goat's front feet wide and square by pushing on the chain. You should also try to always keep all four of your goat's feet square.
